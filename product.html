How to Become a Pool Contractor

You might consider working as a swimming pool contractor if you enjoy being outside. Pool and spa builders and repairers also work on larger pieces of water equipment. Definition of a Pool Contractor & How to Get Swimming Pool License

A swimming pool contractor is defined as “a contractor whose scope of business includes, but is not limited to, the repair and servicing of a swimming pool, hot tub, or spa, whether public or private or otherwise, regardless of use. The scope of work covers the repair or replacement of existing equipment, any cleaning or equipment sanitizing that necessitates at least a partial disassembly (excluding filter changes), and the installation of new pool/spa equipment.
It also covers interior refinishing, the re-installation or addition of pool heaters, the repair or replacement of all perimeter piping and filter piping, and the repair of equipment rooms or housing for pool/spa equipment.

Importance of Swimming Pool License

The use of equipment for the purposes of water treatment or cleaning does not require licensure unless the use involves construction, modification, substantial or complete disassembly, or replacement. However, the installation, construction, modification, substantial or complete disassembly, or replacement of equipment permanently attached to and associated with the pool or spa for those purposes is required. A utility contractor license is not necessary for water treatment that doesn’t use this equipment. Additionally, a license is not necessary if the pool or related equipment is cleaned in a way that doesn’t compromise their structural integrity.
